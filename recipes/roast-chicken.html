Roast Chicken with Schmalzty Cabbage

Description

This is a recipe about a super simple roast chicken.

For the amount of ingredients, honestly I was impressed.

Ingredients

  • 1 large head (2 1/2 pounds) green cabbage
  • Splash of oil, any kind
  • kosher salt
  • 1 whole chicken (shown here is 3 pounds)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • A lemon, if you wish, for serving (we never used ours)
    •

    Steps

    1. Heat your oven to 450 degrees F.
    2. Halve your cabbage and slice each half into 1 to 1.5 inch thick slabs.
    3. Very thinly coat the bottom of a 12-inch ovensafe skillet or an equivalent roasting pan with oil, just to keep the cabbage from sticking before juices trickle down.
    4. Arrange cabbage slices in the pan as if you were making a mosaic, cutting pieces down as needed to get them to fit itghtly.
    5. Season cabbage with salt and pepper.
    6. Pat your chicken dry and rub or brush it with 1 tablespoon of the butter.
    7. Generously salt and pepper the chicken all over (I use a full tablespoon of Diamond kosher salt on my 3-pound bird; use half of another brand).
    8. Place chicken breast side-up over the cabbage and roast for 45 to 60 minutes, spooning the bird and cabbage around it with butter a few times throughout.
    9. Chicken is done when a thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the breast reads 155 degrees, or 165 degrees for the thigh.If your chicken is much larger and you find itâ€™s getting too dark for your tastes, reduce the heat to 425.
    10. Lift the chicken off the cabbage and set on a plate (or warmed tray) to rest.
    11. Flip each section of cabbage over carefully in the pan, nestling them back in, and return the pan to the oven for 5 to 10 minutes at 450 degrees, until the edges are very dark brown.
    12. Season with more salt and pepper if needed.
    13. Cut chicken into peices and serve with the cabbage, finishing everything with lemon if you wish
